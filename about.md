I'm an Applied Scientist at the [Generative AI Innovation Center](https://aws.amazon.com/ai/generative-ai/innovation-center/) at Amazon Web Services. My work sits at the intersection of AI research and real-world deployment: I develop evaluation methodologies and multi-agent architectures that enable enterprises across critical industries to reliably deploy large language models for mission-critical processes.

### Research Interests

- **AI Evaluation**: Designing rigorous methods for assessing generative AI systems, from structured output evaluation for information extraction to confidence calibration in agentic environments
- **Multi-Agent Systems**: Designing and deploying systems that coordinate multiple specialized AI agents to solve complex enterprise problems across industries
- **Synthetic Data for Evaluation**: Establishing validity frameworks for synthetic evaluation data, addressing the fundamental bottleneck of data access in regulated industries
- **LLM Reliability & Calibration**: Two active research threads: (1) quantifying structural consistency in LLM logical reasoning as a measure of uncertainty ([ICLR 2026 Best Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17312)), and (2) pass@k calibration, studying whether LLMs can predict their own marginal gains from additional sampling to enable efficient inference-time compute allocation

### Recent Highlights

- **Best Paper Award** at [ICLR 2026 Workshop on Logical Reasoning of Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17312) for work on quantifying consistency in LLM logical reasoning
- Co-developed [stickler](https://github.com/awslabs/stickler), an open-source evaluation framework for structured LLM outputs (7,000+ monthly PyPI downloads). I'm one of the main contributors to the hierarchical aggregate metrics system for evaluating complex nested structures at arbitrary depth.
- Main author of [Evaluating Intelligent Document Processing Solutions](https://awslabs.github.io/generative-ai-atlas/topics/2_0_technical_foundations_and_patterns/2_6_model_evaluation_and_selection_criteria/2_6_4_domain_specific_evaluations/2_6_4_2_evaluating_intelligent_document_processing/2_6_4_2_evaluating_intelligent_document_processing.html) in the [GenAI Atlas](https://awslabs.github.io/generative-ai-atlas/)
- Organized the AMLC 2025 Workshop on *Evaluation Paradigms for GenAI* (83 paper submissions, 100+ attendees) and organizing the AMLC 2026 Workshop on *Test-Time Scaling: Algorithms, Systems, and Evaluation for Inference-Time Compute in the Era of Reasoning Models*
- AI solutions deployed across 9+ industry sectors: energy, pharmaceutical manufacturing, financial services, media, legal, HR, and consumer retail

### Background

I completed my PhD in Computational Linguistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (alongside an MS in Statistics), where I worked with [Prof. Lane Schwartz](http://dowobeha.github.io/) on computational methods for low-resource languages. My research spanned [multilingual language modeling (TACL 2021)](https://direct.mit.edu/tacl/article/doi/10.1162/tacl_a_00365/98237/Morphology-Matters-A-Multilingual-Language), neural approaches to polysynthetic language modeling at [JSALT 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.05477), and work supported by [NSF](https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1761680). My [dissertation](https://www.ideals.illinois.edu/items/125303) brought much of this work together.

I've been at Amazon since 2022, previously working on LLM-based content generation for Alexa Kids and NLU for Fire TV before joining the GenAI Innovation Center.
